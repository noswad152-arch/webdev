A Light in the Night

No crowd, no crown, no borrowed room
Just borrowed breath in borrowed gloom
The night stayed still, the world stayed blind
He came on time, but out of time

Joseph’s hands are shaking now
Not from fear, but sacred how
You hold the One who made your breath
And teach Him life inside of death

No thunder spoke, no empire knew
He came too small to conquer through

By themselves, beneath the hay
Heaven leaned the other way
No applause, no royal sound
Just God laid helpless on the ground
If this is how You choose to reign
Then break my pride, and break my name

Mary counts each fragile cry
The cost of “yes” still in her eyes
She knows the blade, she knows the road
She holds the weight she hasn’t told

The world outside keeps breathing fine
While God arrives without a sign
No one came to mark the hour
But all of time just lost its power

By themselves, the door shut tight
The Light arrived inside the night
No army formed, no banner waved
Just mercy wrapped in flesh and hay
If this is love, then let it burn
Take my strength—let weakness learn
