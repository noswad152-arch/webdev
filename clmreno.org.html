Insights on prayer from the Christ Love community

Scripture
"Whatsoever ye shall ask the Father in my name, he will give it to you."
John 16:23
The Message

We believe the heart of prayer is revealed with what Jesus says in John 16:23. Prayer spoken this way is not wishful thinking—it is faith in motion.

To pray in His name is to stand in partnership with the living God, trusting that no word shaped by Christ is ever wasted.

To the degree that we know, love, and trust Jesus, our prayers are formed in Him. And when they are, not a single word returns empty.

Because we are divine beings having a human experience, there is always the temptation to pray in our own name—to ask God to bless what we have already decided. By praying in His name we reshape our will to match the Father’s, fulfilling the promise that “it is the Father’s good pleasure to give you the Kingdom” (Luke 12:32).

God hears the substance beneath our words, not the polish of the words themselves. Prayer does not require formality or performance—only honesty. It rises naturally from an authentic heart. True prayer flows from unity rather than division, from trust rather than control.

Prayer does not merely build faith. Prayer is faith. And when we live from that unitive awareness—the living presence of Christ within us—a quiet prayer of gratitude, or a simple “Thy will be done,” may be all the prayer we ever need.
